We are beings dominated and controlled by many things, one of which is hormones. The hormones in our system are like regulators that determine how much we grow and the features we exude. Men generally have androgens while women have estrogens as their hormones, and these are the determinants of the puberty signs that each sex displays.
When women undergo life’s processes like menstruation, menopause, pregnancy, or even childbirth, their estrogen level is affected in the sense that it either becomes low or slightly elevated. If it becomes low, it means the small male hormones that were previously curtailed will now start manifesting, causing our dear sisters to start showcasing male features.
Apart from pregnancy and the other physiological processes I mentioned earlier as the triggers for hormonal fluctuations, stress is another risk factor that makes women experience this disequilibrium. Therefore, we can conclude that women of childbearing age are the most susceptible to having breast hair.
